Through my work at the Benson Center, I’m deeply involved in several outreach efforts. Most importantly, I direct the Benson Undergraduate Fellowship, a fellowship for roughly thirty CU-Boulder undergraduates whose aim is to foster a flourishing intellectual community passionate about the Western tradition, exposure to a wide range of political and other views, and critical reflection on the social, economic, and philosophical questions of our time. As director of this fellowship, I organize and host a variety of co-curricular events for our Fellows and other CU undergraduates, from lunch discussions on artificial intelligence to Western Civilization trivia nights. I am also currently developing a Western Civilization certificate.


Besides directing Benson’s undergraduate fellowship, I also help plan and organize public lectures and other events for the Benson Center. In addition, I play a central role in our strategic planning and actively liaise with donors, university administrators, and other stakeholders.


At DePauw, I mentored undergraduate interns, organized and moderate Courageous Conversations in Ethics, coordinated academic conferences, helped staff Prindlepalooza (an annual event introducing hundreds of DePauw students to ethics initiatives), and more.


Beyond Benson and CU, I’m active in the PPE Society, for which I’ve served as an Annual Meeting co-organizer, referee, session chair, and presenter.
