In my current position at the Prindle Institute for Ethics, I've organized and actively supported a variety of outreach efforts in public philosophy:


  • “The Ethics Of Eating Meat: Courageous Conversations In Ethics,” a moderation conversation on the ethics of eating meat for which I served as a co-discussant
  • Edible Ethics, a series of ethics discussion tables in DePauw's dining hall
  • Fireside Chats, a series of weekly group discussions on ethics with undergraduate student interns and senior residents at a nursing home near DePauw's campus
  • Prindlepalooza,an evening-long event introducing DePauw students to the Prindle Institute's work attended by over 700 people
  • And much more

As Prindle's Assistant Director of Academic Services, I also fulfill a number of other administrative responsibilities, including planning the upcoming Midwest Ethics Symposium, mentoring Prindle's undergraduate Hillman interns, refereeing ethics reading course proposals, and strategic planning.


Beyond DePauw, I’m active in the PPE Society. I’ve served as a co-organizer, referee, and session chair for its Annual Meetings and presented research at these meetings the past four years (most recently on power laundering).
