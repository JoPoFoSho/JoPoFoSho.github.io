PPE


I’m heavily involved in UNC’s PPE Program, in which I’m an Affiliated Graduate Student. I’ve organized and hosted several PPE events at UNC:


  • “Beyond Policy: Human Perspectives on the Opioid Crisis,” a daylong interdisciplinary workshop on the opioid crisis in collaboration with Carolina Public Humanities
  • “What Can We Learn from ‘GameStopGate’?”, a PPE salon on the ethics and politics of retail trading
  • “Bitcoin: What Does the Future Hold?”, a PPE salon on the politics and potential long-term impact of bitcoin

Additionally, I’ve taught UNC’s PPE Gateway course as the instructor of record (and a similar course at Penn as a teaching assistant), led a PPE reading group, completed graduate coursework in PPE, and served on a PPE hiring committee.


Beyond UNC, I’m also active in the PPE Society. I’ve served as a co-organizer, referee, and session chair for its Annual Meetings (including the upcoming meeting in October). I’ve also presented my own research at these meetings in 2019 and 2020. My 2019 presentation, “Explaining Normative Drift,” offered a rebuttal to Michael Huemer’s empirical case for moral realism. My 2020 presentation, “Meat Me Halfway? Health and the Ethics of Animal-based Diets,” argued in favor of the ethical permissibility of animal-based diets under certain circumstances.
